Designing Personality for a Digital Storefront

The owners of 45th Parallel Wines, a brick‑and‑mortar shop in Portland’s St. John’s neighborhood, asked me to design their online presence using Shopify. While the site leveraged a template, the transformation came through the bespoke brand and illustration ecosystem I created.

I developed custom illustrations intentionally rooted in St. John’s geography and landmarks—from the iconic bridge to neighborhood streetscapes, so the digital storefront carried a sense of place unique to their community. Alongside these illustrations, I crafted a logo, cohesive layout, and a typography and color system that gave the brand a distinctive personality.

Through moodboards, client conversations, and iterative brainstorming, I translated their vision into a clean, minimalist yet playful identity. CSS tweaks ensured the design felt tailored rather than generic, while the brand system extended naturally to copy, content, and potential touchpoints like signage and social media.

Design That Grows With the Brand

The site was built with responsive layouts, ensuring seamless performance across desktop, tablet, and mobile. I also created an extensible illustration system: thin‑line, flat‑style drawings built from simple geometric shapes referencing stick figures. Designed for flexibility and reuse, these illustrations became a signature identity element that could adapt to web banners, social media, packaging, or event collateral. This approach showcased both my branding craft and my ability to design systems that scale and evolve with the business.
